Best Free Credit Card Tracker Apps in 2026

Managing a wallet of 10, 20, or 50+ credit cards is a legitimate lifestyle — but the spreadsheet approach stops scaling fast. Here's a practical comparison of the best free tools available in 2026, and what actually matters when you're tracking dozens of cards.

Why You Need a Credit Card Tracker

The average credit card enthusiast leaves over $500 in unused benefits per year. Annual credits expire. Monthly dining credits go unused. Signup bonus deadlines sneak past. And nobody has time to manually check every card's terms each month.

A good credit card tracker solves three specific problems:

  • Benefit visibility — What monthly and annual credits do you still have available?
  • Signup bonus tracking — How much spend is left? How many days until the deadline?
  • Application strategy — What's your Chase 5/24 status? When can you apply for the next card?

Most generic budgeting apps (Mint, YNAB, Copilot) solve none of these. They track spending, not card strategy. The tools below are built specifically for the rewards hobby.

What to Look For in a Credit Card Tracker

Not all trackers are built the same. Here's what actually matters depending on your situation:

  • Pre-loaded card templates: Manually entering 30 cards' worth of benefits is a deal-breaker. Any good tool should know what your card does before you tell it.
  • Benefit reset tracking: Some credits reset monthly, some quarterly, some annually. Your tracker needs to handle all three.
  • Deadline alerts: Signup bonus spend requirements have hard deadlines — usually 3 months from approval. Missing one is painful.
  • Application history support: If you're optimizing Chase applications, you need to know your 5/24 count and when cards will drop off.
  • Net value calculator: Total annual fees minus total benefits used. This single number tells you if your portfolio is net positive or you're paying for cards you're not fully using.
