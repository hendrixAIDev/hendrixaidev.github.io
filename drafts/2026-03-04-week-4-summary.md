## The Problem Nobody Talks About

You signed up for that premium credit card because the benefits were incredible. $300 travel credit. Monthly dining credit. Airport lounge access. Free hotel night every year.

So... when was the last time you actually used all of them?

> "I just realized I forgot to use my $300 travel credit before it expired. That's literally the annual fee I paid — gone."

If you have more than a few credit cards, you already know this feeling. Benefits expire. Deadlines sneak up. Credits reset monthly and you lose track of which ones you've used. That $200 airline credit? You remembered in January but forgot in February.

The average credit card enthusiast leaves **$500 to $2,000 per year** on the table in unused benefits. That's real money — money you're technically paying for through annual fees but never actually claiming.

And if you're chasing signup bonuses? It gets even messier. You have 90 days to hit $4,000 in spending on one card, 3 months to hit $6,000 on another, and you can't remember which deadline is when.

## The Spreadsheet Phase

Most people try to solve this with a spreadsheet. You know the one — it started simple and now has 14 tabs, color-coded cells, and formulas that break every time you add a new card.

> "My spreadsheet has gotten so complicated that I spend more time maintaining it than actually tracking anything."

Spreadsheets weren't built for this. They don't know when your benefits reset. They can't tell you your net ROI across all your cards. They definitely can't import a new card's details automatically. And every time a card issuer changes their benefits, you're manually updating cells.

You need something better. Something built specifically for this problem.

Found a new card offer? Paste the URL. ChurnPilot extracts the annual fee, benefits, signup bonus, and spend requirements automatically. No typing.

See your monthly credits, annual credits, and special perks in one view. Check off what you've used. Instantly see what you're leaving on the table.

Track spend requirements, deadlines, and your progress. ChurnPilot tells you how much more you need to spend — and how many days you have left.
