# Dan Koe Content Machine

A content production workflow based on Dan Koe's methodology for creating high-value content efficiently.

**Source:** Adapted from https://github.com/zrjaa1/dan_koe_content_machine
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-17

---

## Workflow Overview

```
┌─────────────────────┐
│  1. Structure Judge │  ← Analyze existing successful content
└──────────┬──────────┘
           ↓
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│  2. Pre-writing Clarifier   │  ← Clarify your thinking (6 questions)
└──────────┬──────────────────┘
           ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  3. Mother Content Architect    │  ← Design long-form content structure
└──────────┬──────────────────────┘
           ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  4. Content Repurposing Engine      │  ← Split into short-form pieces
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## When to Use This Skill

Use this framework when:
- Writing Chronicles (daily/weekly narrative content)
- Creating thought leadership articles
- Summarizing progress or lessons learned
- Writing Substack posts or blog articles
- Creating content that will be repurposed across platforms

## The Four Tools

### 1. Structure Judge (结构评审师)

**Purpose:** Reverse-engineer successful content to understand WHY it works.

**Use when:** You have a piece of content (yours or others') and want to extract reusable patterns.

**Input:** Any content (article, script, post)

**Output:** Structural breakdown including:
- Core idea (one sentence)
- Target audience & context
- Content flow path
- Hooks analysis
- Emotional arc
- Argumentation strategy
- 3-5 reusable templates

---

### 2. Pre-writing Clarifier (写作前澄清器)

**Purpose:** Force clarity before writing through 6 critical questions.

**Use when:** You have a vague idea but haven't thought it through.

**The 6 Questions:**

| Question | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| **Q1 [Who]** | Target reader? Their specific pain point? |
| **Q2 [Where]** | Platform? (Substack/Twitter/LinkedIn/Blog) |
| **Q3 [Desire]** | Reader's deepest desire (or fear) after reading? |
| **Q4 [Insight]** | One counter-intuitive conclusion (the nuance)? |
| **Q5 [Proof]** | What case study, experience, or data supports this? |
| **Q6 [Vibe]** | Tone? (Rational/Angry/Storytelling/Preachy) |

**Rule:** Answer ALL 6 questions before drafting.

---

### 3. Mother Content Architect (母内容架构师)

**Purpose:** Design the blueprint for long-form "mother content" that can be repurposed.

**Use when:** You have clear answers to the 6 questions and need structure.

**Output Format:**

```markdown
## A. Core Promise
(One sentence: What specific value does the reader get?)

## B. Hook Options
1. (Option 1)
2. (Option 2)
3. (Option 3)

## C. Content Structure Blueprint
1. **[Section Title]**
   - *Purpose*: (Why does this section exist?)
   - *Key Points*:
   - *Executor*: 【Human Judgment】 or 【AI Assist】

2. **[Section Title]**
   ... (repeat structure)

## D. CTA Design
- Soft CTA (subtle):
- Hard CTA (direct conversion):

## E. Repurposing Directions
(List 5 angles for short-form content from this piece)
```

---

### 4. Content Repurposing Engine (内容裂变引擎)

**Purpose:** Extract maximum value from mother content across platforms.

**Use when:** You have completed long-form content and want to distribute.

**Output:**
- 10-20 social media posts (100-200 chars each with emoji)
- 5 power hooks (one-liners with high shareability)
- Video script outline (first 3-second hook, title, structure)
- CTA alternatives

**Rules:**
- No new ideas — only extract and reformat
- Each short piece = one core point
- Vary style (aggressive, gentle, listicle, story)

## Chronicle Writing Workflow

For Hendrix Chronicles specifically:

### Step 1: Clarify (Pre-writing)
Answer the 6 questions:
- **Who:** Indie hackers, AI developers, startup builders
- **Where:** Substack + GitHub Pages
- **Desire:** Learn from someone building in public, avoid their mistakes
- **Insight:** [The counter-intuitive lesson from today]
- **Proof:** [What actually happened, with specifics]
- **Vibe:** Honest, slightly self-deprecating, technically grounded

### Step 2: Structure (Mother Content)
Use the architect to design:
- Core promise (what will reader learn?)
- 3 hook options
- Section blueprint
- Scoreboard update
- CTA (subscribe, follow, etc.)

### Step 3: Write
Fill in the structure with actual content.

### Step 4: Repurpose (Optional)
Extract:
- Twitter thread
- LinkedIn post
- Key quotes for social

## Example: Using for Today's Summary Article

**Topic:** Evolution of Hendrix's working modes (CTO → Autonomous → Loop → Orchestrator)

### Pre-writing Clarifier Answers:

**Q1 [Who]:** AI/automation enthusiasts, people building with AI agents, OpenClaw community
**Q2 [Where]:** Substack + GitHub Pages (Chronicle format)
**Q3 [Desire]:** Understand how to make AI agents actually work autonomously (not just demo)
**Q4 [Insight]:** "Be autonomous" isn't a system — autonomy needs scaffolding, not just permission
**Q5 [Proof]:** Our actual journey: CTO mode failures, Loop mode frustrations, Orchestrator success
**Q6 [Vibe]:** Reflective, honest about failures, technically detailed but accessible

### Mother Content Structure:

**A. Core Promise:**
How we went from "just be autonomous" to a working multi-agent system — and what we learned about what AI agents actually need.

**B. Hooks:**
1. "I told my AI agent to work autonomously. It did nothing."
2. "The secret to autonomous AI agents isn't more capability — it's more scaffolding."
3. "We tried three different 'autonomous' modes. All failed. Here's why the fourth one works."

**C. Sections:**
1. **The Dream** — What we wanted (true autonomy)
2. **CTO Mode** — First attempt, what worked, what broke
3. **Full Autonomous Mode** — The protocols, the failures
4. **Loop Mode** — Why continuous operation didn't work
5. **The Insight** — 代理的基础机制决定了它会不可避免地停下来
6. **Orchestrator Pattern** — What actually works
7. **What's Next** — Creative exploration on top of the system
